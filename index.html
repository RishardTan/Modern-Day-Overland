Wallace Line, boundary between the Oriental and Australian faunal regions, proposed by the 19th-century British naturalist Alfred Russel Wallace. The line extends from the Indian Ocean through the Lombok Strait (between the islands of Bali and Lombok), northward through the Makassar Strait (between Borneo and Celebes), and eastward, south of Mindanao, into the Philippine Sea. Although many zoogeographers no longer consider the Wallace Line to be a regional boundary, it does represent an abrupt limit of distribution for many major animal groups. Many fish, bird, and mammal groups are abundantly represented on one side of the Wallace Line but poorly or not at all on the other side.
-Encyclopaedia Britannica 2020-



Architecture of Indonesia stretches from Sabang in the West to Merauke in the East which is also crossed by the Equator so climate, customs and traditions become very diverse which greatly influences the development process of vernacular architecture in Indonesia. Houses on the West side are different from the houses on the East where the humidity levels are also very diverse because geographically we are affected by the Australian climate, especially in Eastern part of Indonesia.

Background

In early 2013, the three of us wanted to take a short vacation to get out of our daily activities which are too monotonous. We are alumnis of a university in Bandung, majoring in architecture. We have different business in different places, causing our enthusiasm to build up in arranging trips to destinations to each of which we have never visited before by bringing our vehicles that are used in daily bases. Eastern Indonesia as our first trip destination was agreed with fourteen-day corridor before returning back to each others routine.

We chose the Eastern part of Indonesia because we have different flora and fauna with the island of Java where we live beside culture and traditional houses which are architecturally different because of the climate. With time and schedule constraints to plan together, this trip will only be carried out in August-September 2015.

The basic concept of this trip is that we will use local lodging facilities at the place where we stop or stay during night times but there is always an option to stay for a camp when the scenery are very attractive and possible to make a camp stay during the overnight. Equipments required for these activities must be prepared from the beginning. In these trips we want to get to our daily destination before dark so we can enjoy the sunset and also the atmosphere around, we also set the travel time around 6-7 hours each day.
This site was meant to remember our journey and friendship.
